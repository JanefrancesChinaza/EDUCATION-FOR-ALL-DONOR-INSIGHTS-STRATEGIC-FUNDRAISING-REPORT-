# EDUCATION-FOR-ALL-DONOR-INSIGHTS-STRATEGIC-FUNDRAISING-REPORT-

 Project Overview
This project focuses on leveraging data analytics to support the   "Education-for-All"   initiative, which provides underprivileged children with access to quality education. By analyzing historical donor behavior and donation patterns, this project aims to identify strategic opportunities to maximize fundraising efforts, optimize contributions from existing formal donors, and discover effective pathways to attract new supporters.

Business Objectives
Analyze Donor Behavior: Identify trends in donation frequency, high-value donor demographics, and giving cycles. 
Optimize Retention: Uncover insights on how to keep existing donors engaged and increase their lifetime value.
Acquisition Strategy: Formulate data-backed recommendations to target and acquire new donor segments.
Executive Presentation: Translate complex query results into high-level, actionable recommendations for organizational leadership.

 Tech Stack & Skills Demonstrated
SQL (PostgreSQL): Advanced querying, data aggregation, filtering, and cohort analysis.
Data-Driven Storytelling: Translating raw database tables into executive-level strategic summaries.
Stakeholder Communication: Building professional slide decks and reports tailored for non-technical leadership.

Key Findings
The findings show that while individual donation amounts vary, a significant portion of our capital—over 65% ($162,350.00)—is driven by a high-value tier of donors giving more than $200 per transaction, despite this group making up a minority of total interactions (410 donations). From a sector perspective, corporate employees working in Business Development and Engineering represent our highest-grossing donor networks, bringing in $42,150.00 and $38,900.00 respectively. Geographically, performance is heavily concentrated in major metropolitan hubs led by California ($34,500.00) and New York ($29,100.00), while smaller states like Wyoming yield negligible returns. Additionally, cross-referencing lifestyle preferences revealed a profound correlation between high-value giving and owners of premium car makes such as Tesla, BMW, and Mercedes-Benz.
